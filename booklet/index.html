6-Month Journey Calendar

Month 1

"Hello World" ‚Äî Building Connection

Week 1-2: Rapport building, baseline assessment, sensory exploration
Week 3-4: Parent orientation, basic engagement activities
‚úì Milestone: Child comfortable with therapists, baseline documented
Month 2

"My First Words" ‚Äî Vocabulary Basics

Week 1: Family words (mama, papa, dadi, dada)
Week 2: Need words (khana, pani, doodh, nahi, haan)
Week 3: Action words (khao, pio, aao, jao)
Week 4: Object words (ball, gaadi, kitab, kapde)
‚úì Milestone: 10-15 word attempts, gesture+word pairing
Month 3

"Sounds Around Me" ‚Äî Articulation

Week 1: Environmental sounds (animal sounds, vehicles)
Week 2: Vowel sound practice through songs
Week 3: Early consonants (Ma, Pa, Ba, Da)
Week 4: Sound combinations in simple words
‚úì Milestone: 3-5 clear consonant sounds, improved oral motor control
Month 4

"Two Words Together" ‚Äî Phrase Building

Week 1: More + noun ("aur doodh", "aur ball")
Week 2: Action + object ("khao khana", "pio pani")
Week 3: Person + action ("mama aao", "papa dekho")
Week 4: Modifier + noun ("bada ball", "chhota gaadi")
‚úì Milestone: 5-10 two-word combinations used spontaneously
Month 5

"Tell Me More" ‚Äî Expression

Week 1: Requesting sentences ("mujhe ___ chahiye")
Week 2: Refusing/negating ("nahi chahiye")
Week 3: Commenting ("dekho ___", "yeh hai ___")
Week 4: Questioning basics ("kya hai?", "kahan hai?")
‚úì Milestone: Simple 3-word sentences, expresses needs verbally
Month 6

"Talking with Friends" ‚Äî Social Communication

Week 1: Greetings (namaste, hello, bye-bye)
Week 2: Peer interaction basics, sharing language
Week 3: Conversation skills, topic maintenance
Week 4: Progress review, celebration, transition planning
‚úì Milestone: 50+ functional words, greets familiar people, peer interaction emerging

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my child need a diagnosis to join?

No formal diagnosis is required. If you have concerns about your child's development, that's enough to start. Our assessment will help identify the right program.

How do I know which program is right?

Our free consultation and comprehensive assessment will guide the recommendation. Our experts will evaluate your child and suggest the best-fit program.

What if my child doesn't cooperate?

We're trained to engage reluctant learners using play-based, child-centered approaches. Most children come to enjoy sessions within weeks.

How is this different from tuition?

Regular tuition teaches curriculum. We address underlying developmental challenges using specialized therapeutic techniques, not just teaching methods.

Will this cure my child's condition?

We focus on skill-building and measurable progress, not "cures." Every child can learn and grow with the right support.

Do you coordinate with schools?

Yes, with your permission. We provide progress reports and strategy recommendations for teachers.

What if 6 months isn't enough?

Many children continue with extended or maintenance programs. We plan together based on progress.

How involved do parents need to be?

Parent involvement is crucial. We provide weekly training and daily 10-15 minute home practice activities.

Can we visit before enrolling?

Absolutely! Book a free consultation to visit our center, meet our team, and see our approach.

What are your operating hours?

Monday to Saturday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Sessions scheduled based on availability and child's needs.

Do you provide transportation?

Currently, parents need to bring children to our center. We're a home-based center in a residential area.

What if we need to take a break?

Short breaks (1-2 weeks) can be accommodated. Longer breaks may require re-enrollment based on availability.
